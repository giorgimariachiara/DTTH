38 years ago, Giuseppe Fava, known as Pippo, was killed with five shots
from a 7.65 calibre pistol in front of the Teatro Stabile in Catania.
He was a journalist, writer, playwright, essayist and screenwriter.
Born in the province of Syracuse in 1925, he became a professional journalist in 1952.

In 1956 he was hired by 'Espresso sera' where he remained until 1980 as
editor-in-chief. In 1980, he moved to the direction of a new editorial
reality: the 'Giornale del Sud'. Fava immediately created a young and
fierce team with which he tackled the burning issues of Sicily in those
years. Building speculation, the ramification of Mafia connivance in
society. Il Giornale is irreverent, fearlessly denouncing the intermingling
of political power and mafia associations, building speculation, drug
trafficking and o-pposing the creation of the Comiso missile base
His conviction is that Sicily is a metaphor for Italy.
These themes have also characterised his work as a writer and scriptwriter,
which began in his days at 'Espresso sera'. He wrote novels, plays and
collaborated on scripts for television and film, often based on his novels.
He hosts a radio programme on 'Radiorai'. The topics of the 'Giornale del Sud'
annoyed, after only a year the ownership of the newspaper changed hands, it
was then discovered that the new editors were linked to mafia circles in Catania.
Giuseppe Fava is fired and shortly afterwards the newspaper closes.

Io ho un concetto etico di giornalismo.
Ritengo infatti che in una società democratica
e libera quale dovrebbe essere quella italiana,
il giornalismo rappresenti la forza essenziale della società.
A che serve vivere, se non c’è coraggio di lottare?

Giuseppe non si arrende, fonda 'Radar' una cooperativa con i giornalisti della
vecchia redazione del Giornale che gli sono rimasti più vicino. A Novembre del
1982 esce il primo numero della rivista che avrà cadenza mensile: ‘I Siciliani’.
È subito un punto di riferimento per il movimento antimafia. Le inchieste della
rivista diventano spesso un caso politico e giornalistico. Giuseppe Fava firma un
articolo in cui collega quattro grandi imprenditori catanesi al clan del boss Nitto Santapaola.
L'anno successivo, gli stessi imprenditori, cercarono di comprare il giornale per poterlo
controllare, ottenendo solo rifiuti. Il 28 dicembre 1983 rilascia la sua ultima intervista a Enzo Biagi.

He was killed in an ambush one rainy evening on 5 January 1984 in Via dello Stadio.
He had left the editorial office and was on his way to the Verga Theatre to pick up his
59-year-old niece. He did not even have time to get out of the car. Initially the crime was
labelled as a crime of passion, later it was said to be linked to the economic problems of the
Radar cooperative. Only later is it linked to the world of organised crime. And it was only in
2001 that Nitto Santapaola and Aldo Ercolano had their life sentences confirmed by the Catania
Court of Appeal, characters of a certain importance in the Cosa Nostra in Catania.
