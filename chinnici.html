About
Rocco Chinnici was an Italian magistrate. His name is linked to the idea of the establishment of "anti-Mafia pool", which gave a decisive breakthrough in the fight against Cosa Nostra. This was an area in which he is considered one of the most important personalities, along with Antonino Caponnetto, Giovanni Falcone and Paolo Borsellino.In 1970 he was assigned the case of the so-called "Viale Lazio massacre," which included many names of Mafia criminals destined for later greater notoriety. In 1975, having reached the rank of Court of Appeals magistrate, he was appointed Deputy Instructing Counsel. He became a magistrate of the Court of Cassation and Instructing Councilor after another four years, and as such, in that 1979 when Cesare Terranova was killed, he was called to the post of head of the office in which he was After other murders Chinnici had the idea of establishing a collaborative structure among the magistrates of the Office (later known as the anti-Mafia pool), aware that the isolation of servants of the state exposes them to annihilation and makes them vulnerable, particularly judges and policemen since, by killing those who investigate alone, one also buries with them the fruits of their investigations. Some young magistrates including Giovanni Falcone and Paolo Borsellino joined his team. already working on that "excellent" crime.In July 1982, the so-called "Report of 162" arrived on Chinnici's desk , drawn up jointly by the Police and Carabinieri that highlighted, for the first time, the Mafia alignments involved in the then ongoing Second Mafia War, both the "losing" (the Bontate-Inzerillo-Badalamenti faction) and "winning" (Tot√≤ Riina's Corleonesi) groups, and the related murders with scrupulous verification and matching. Chinnici decided to entrust the inquiry concerning the investigation to Judge Falcone, and the result of that impressive work would be the first major trial of Cosa Nostra, the so-called Palermo maxi-trial. Rocco Chinnici was killed at 8 a.m. on the 29th of July of 1983 in a green Fiat 126, stuffed with 75 kilograms of explosives parked in front of his home on Via Giuseppe Pipitone Federico in Palermo, at the age of fifty-eight. Operating the remote control that caused the explosion was Antonino Madonia, boss of Resuttana, who was hiding in the trunk of a stolen van parked nearby.
